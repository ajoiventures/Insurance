Elevated risk on Geovera:
  • Geovera is a non-admitted / surplus lines carrier — not backed by FIGA (Florida's guaranty fund for insolvent insurers). Standard-market protection doesn't apply.
  • Policy fees, underwriting fees, and inspection fees are fully earned — non-refundable even if the policy is cancelled early.
  • Water damage from burst pipes/accidental discharge is excluded — a meaningful gap for an older home with prior lapse and (per the 4-point/wind mitigation files) polybutylene plumbing.
  • Requires a post-bind exterior/roof inspection; approval isn't guaranteed until that's done.
  • Carrier is A-rated by AM Best, and the policy does flag the standard landlord clause that tenant-caused damage liability is reduced — worth requiring renters insurance from your tenant as the carrier suggests.
Citizens caveats: Citizens is Florida's state-backed insurer of last resort — admitted market, no non-refundable-fee issue like Geovera — but the $4,190 indication is preliminary with no dwelling/contents/deductible breakdown yet, doesn't include liability (~$500–700/yr extra, pushing all-in cost to ~$4,690–4,890), and carries a 30-day wait before coverage starts. On price alone Geovera ($3,365, liability included) is still cheaper even after that math, but Citizens avoids Geovera's water-damage exclusion and non-admitted status — worth getting the full policy terms before ruling it out.
Recommendation: this property has been uninsured since 2022, so closing that gap is the priority. You now have three concrete options. Geovera ($3,365, all-in incl. liability) is a broader "special form" policy but excludes water damage and is a non-admitted surplus carrier. Citizens ($4,190 + ~$500–700 liability ≈ $4,690–4,890 all-in) is the priciest and slowest to bind (30-day wait) but is the standard admitted market.

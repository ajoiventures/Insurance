Geovera's $8,000 Other Structures limit is very unlikely to cover what's actually on this property: a pool, a 500 sq ft screened enclosure, and a detached 2-car garage. American Integrity's $48,000 limit is a much better match for that exposure — but its DP1 "Dwelling Basic" form is a bare-bones named-perils policy that excludes all personal property, so it isn't a like-for-like comparison to Geovera's broader DP3 form. Frontline and American Traditions both default to the standard 2% allocation ($11,320 and $10,120) — same underinsurance concern as Geovera; Burns & Wilcox and Monarch are even lower at $7,500 and $7,000. A single storm-damaged screened enclosure alone commonly costs $15,000–$30,000 to rebuild in Florida.
Two more items worth flagging: American Traditions excludes water damage and puts the roof on a payment schedule (likely ACV rather than full replacement cost) — a second quote making the same water-damage trade-off as Tower Hill did for Cider Mill. And the Frontline quote lists the named insured as "the applicant entity" rather than the LLC — worth confirming with Manuel whether that's a data entry issue or an actual different entity, since getting this wrong on the application could affect coverage validity.

Two more Sihle quotes just came in: Burns & Wilcox (Markel/Evanston Insurance Co, surplus lines, $3,490.16/yr) correctly names "the LLC" as the insured — unlike Frontline's LLC mix-up — but is dominated by Geovera: $45,000 less dwelling coverage, less than half the personal property limit, and a higher 3%/$11,250 wind deductible for a similar price, plus the same water-damage exclusion. Monarch ($4,722/yr, admitted) is the most expensive quote on the property yet has the lowest dwelling limit ($350,000) of any option — its only edge is a small $10,000 water-damage buy-back, not enough to offset the price and coverage gap versus Frontline or American Traditions.

Also on file: Florida Insurance Group (Elizabeth Sito, a different producer at the same Lee Rogers Agency Gabriel works at) sent a second, separate Geovera quote — #QD39053005, $3,391.48/yr, Dwelling $420,000, same water-damage exclusion and roof payment-schedule limitation as the Rigetta Geovera quote above. It's effectively the same policy through a different retail channel, priced slightly higher — dominated by the existing Geovera quote and not shown as its own column above.
